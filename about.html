How Macro Counter Works

To use a Macro count for weight gain, its important to note that a pound is equivalent to 3,000 calories respectively.

The key is really in 'how' you get these 3,000 calories. They should primarily come from: Protien, Fat & Carbs.

To calculate this, you need three things:

Protein: Take in 1g of protein per pound of body weight. To get the calories for this, the calculation would be: number of lbs x 1g(protein) x 4(calories per gram of protein)

Fat: Take in .4g of fat per pound of body weight. To get calories for this, the calculation would be: number of lbs x .4g(fat) x 9(calories per gram of fat)

Carbs: The remainder of your calories will come from carbs. To get both the calories and carb count needed would be: protein calories + fat calories - 3000(calories needed) / 4

The Macro Counter simplifies this process by performing all of the calculations for you by simply entering you current weight.
